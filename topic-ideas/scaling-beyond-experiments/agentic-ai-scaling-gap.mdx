### Angle

McKinsey reports **62% of organisations are experimenting with AI agents** — but fewer than **10% are actually scaling them**. The vast majority are deploying yesterday's chatbot while calling it an AI strategy. This is the pilot trap in its next form: different technology, same failure pattern.

### Why It Works for Kate

Kate's entire "Scaling Beyond Experiments" pillar is built on the insight that the gap between pilot and production is a leadership and operating model problem, not a technical one. This McKinsey data gives her a fresh, specific manifestation of that pattern. She can name the mechanism: companies are treating agents as experiments (low commitment, no workflow redesign) when agents require *full process reinvention* to generate value.

No generic AI commentator is framing the agent adoption gap through a CEO/operating model lens. That's Kate's edge.

## Raw McKinsey Signal

| Finding | Data Point |
|---|---|
| Organisations experimenting with AI agents | 62% |
| Organisations scaling AI agents | <10% |
| Product dev teams not using agents at all | 73% |
| High performers redesigning workflows for agentic AI | ~3× more likely than peers |

**Kate's lens**: High performers aren't just deploying agents — they're redesigning the work *around* agents. That's an operating model decision, not a technology decision. The organisations stuck in experimentation are waiting for the technology to do the work of transformation for them. It won't.
